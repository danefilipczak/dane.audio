Much of the harmonic language that interests me is characterized by direct relationships between chords, often without reference to an overarching scale or key center. Contemporary music theorists have used geometry to study and describe the myriad ways in which chords can be related to one another. In order to make these abstract relationships more intuitive, I'm developing interfaces for their exploration. The first is Lufttonnetz , a 'flight simulator' that allows one to navigate the Neo-Riemannian Tonnetz.
